MASHED POTATOES
SERVES 6
This recipe delivers spectacularly smooth, fluffy potatoes, not too rich and not too assertively flavored. Russet potatoes make slightly fluffier mashed potatoes, but Yukon Golds have an appealing buttery flavor. This recipe can be doubled.

2 pounds russet or Yukon Gold potatoes, peeled, eyes and blemishes removed, cut into 2-inch chunks

3/4 teaspoon salt

6 tablespoons butter, softened

1 cup whole milk or half-and-half, warm

Ground black pepper

1. Put potatoes in a large saucepan; add cold water to cover and 1/2 teaspoon salt. Bring to boil and continue to cook over medium heat until potatoes are tender when pierced with a knife, 15 to 20 minutes.

2. Drain potatoes well and return pan to low heat. Rice potatoes into pan and use whisk or wooden spoon to blend in butter, then warm milk. Or, return potatoes to saucepan; mash over low heat with a potato masher, adding butter as you mash and stirring in warm milk.

3. Season mashed potatoes with 1/4 teaspoon salt and a pinch of black pepper, and serve immediately.
